Historical Events Timeline

Fourth Turning (Crisis)
A decisive era of upheaval when institutional life is destroyed and rebuilt in response to a perceived threat to the nation's survival. Examples include the American Revolution, Civil War, and World War II.
Third Turning (Unraveling)
A downcast era of strengthening individualism and weakening institutions, when the old civic order decays and the new values regime implants. Similar to the 1990s and early 2000s.
Second Turning (Awakening)
A passionate era of spiritual upheaval, when the civic order comes under attack from a new values regime. Examples include the Progressive Era and the "Consciousness Revolution" of the 1960s.
First Turning (High)
An upbeat era of strengthening institutions and weakening individualism, when a new civic order implants and the old values regime decays. Examples include the post-World War II American High.
